India: International Briefing

Report date: December 19, 2024

The Indian government has initiated a comprehensive social reform programme targeting the elimination of child marriages nationwide. The Ministry of Women and Child Development launched the 'Bal Vivah Mukt Bharat' (Child Marriage Free India) mission on November 27, 2024, establishing an ambitious timeline to eliminate the practice by 2047 [6]. This initiative aligns with the Prime Minister's vision of a developed India and operates under constitutional provisions including Article 21, whilst drawing support from existing legislation through the Prohibition of Child Marriage Act of 2006 [6].

The mission targets compliance with international development frameworks, specifically supporting Sustainable Development Goal 5.3, which seeks the elimination of harmful practices including child, early, and forced marriages by 2030 [6]. Girls from marginalised communities are disproportionately impacted by child marriage practices, making them a particular focus of the government initiative [6]. The programme demonstrates coordination between domestic constitutional frameworks and international development commitments.

Economic data remains limited in available reporting, with insufficient information to assess current trade relationships, labour market conditions, or broader economic indicators. The focus on social reform through the child marriage elimination programme represents the most significant documented policy development, positioned as a component of long-term national development objectives extending to 2047.

Social challenges persist across various sectors, with documented concerns regarding women's safety in public spaces. Sexual harassment and assault incidents frequently occur in crowded areas including marketplaces, train stations, and buses [5]. These incidents of verbal and physical harassment are known locally as 'Eve-teasing' [5]. Such safety concerns affect both domestic populations and international visitors to the country.

Cultural practices continue to demonstrate regional diversity and traditional continuity. Traditional attire such as sarees remain commonly worn by women across different areas [5]. Dietary customs vary significantly by region and cultural background, with rice serving as a staple food in southern areas of the country [5]. These practices reflect the persistence of established cultural norms alongside ongoing social changes.

Cricket maintains its established position as a national passion, alongside cinema, indicating the continued prominence of these entertainment forms in Indian society [5]. However, comprehensive information about current developments in popular culture, sports achievements, or entertainment industry activities remains limited in available documentation.

The child marriage elimination initiative represents the most substantial documented policy commitment, reflecting governmental capacity for addressing complex social issues through legislative and administrative coordination. The programme's integration of constitutional provisions, existing legislation, and international development goals demonstrates institutional frameworks for long-term social reform implementation.

Key Developments

• Government launched 'Bal Vivah Mukt Bharat' mission in November 2024 targeting elimination of child marriages by 2047

• Initiative operates under constitutional Article 21 and Prohibition of Child Marriage Act 2006 framework

• Programme specifically addresses vulnerabilities of girls from marginalised communities

• Mission aligns with UN Sustainable Development Goal 5.3 targeting harmful practice elimination by 2030

• Sexual harassment incidents continue to occur frequently in crowded public spaces

• Traditional cultural practices including cricket enthusiasm and regional dress customs persist
