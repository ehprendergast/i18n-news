Philippines: International Briefing

Report date: December 19, 2024

Ferdinand Marcos Jr. serves as President of the Philippines following a landslide victory in May 2022 [8]. The president, commonly known by the nickname "Bongbong," succeeded Rodrigo Duterte, who had governed since 2016 [8]. Sara Duterte serves as vice-president and is the daughter of former president Rodrigo Duterte [8]. This political arrangement unites two populist right-wing dynasties [8].

The constitution was modeled after foreign systems, following the country's achievement of independence in 1946 [8]. The Philippines had been under Spanish colonial rule for over three centuries before this transition [8]. The constitution guarantees press freedom, though the Philippines ranks among the most dangerous countries globally for journalists [8].

Economic data remains limited in available reporting. The archipelago's strategic positioning provides maritime borders with Vietnam, Taiwan, Palau, Malaysia, and Indonesia [9]. The country covers approximately 300,000 square kilometers across more than 7,000 islands [8][9].

The Philippines consists of more than 7,000 islands, though most of its approximately 109 million people live on just 11 islands [8]. The archipelago is divided into three main regions: Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao [9]. Luzon is the largest and most populous island in the northern region [9]. The country has approximately 20 active volcanoes and is frequently affected by typhoons [8].

Filipino and English serve as official languages alongside regional languages [8]. Life expectancy is 70 years for men and 74 years for women [8]. The influence of over three centuries of Spanish colonial rule continues to shape cultural patterns across the islands [8].

Television broadcasting is dominated by free-to-air networks ABS-CBN and GMA [8]. Commercial interests control much of the media content [8]. Despite constitutional guarantees of press freedom, journalists operate in conditions that rank the Philippines among the world's most dangerous countries for media professionals [8].

Limited information is available regarding popular culture, sports, or entertainment sectors beyond the television broadcasting landscape. The media environment reflects the broader challenges facing press freedom in the archipelago nation.

The geographic and demographic concentration of population across the island chain continues to influence political and economic dynamics. Natural disasters from volcanic activity and typhoons represent ongoing challenges for the densely populated areas [8].

The political alliance between the Marcos and Duterte families represents a significant consolidation of populist right-wing influence following the 2022 electoral cycle. This arrangement reflects the continuing importance of political dynasties in Philippine governance structures.

Key Developments

• Ferdinand Marcos Jr. assumed the presidency in 2022 through a landslide electoral victory, forming a political alliance with the Duterte family through Sara Duterte's vice-presidency

• The consolidation unites two populist right-wing dynasties following Rodrigo Duterte's presidency since 2016

• The archipelago's 109 million residents remain concentrated on just 11 of more than 7,000 islands across three main regions

• Press freedom faces significant challenges despite constitutional protections, with the Philippines ranking among the world's most dangerous countries for journalists

• Television broadcasting continues under the dominance of ABS-CBN and GMA networks, with commercial interests controlling much media content

• Natural hazards from approximately 20 active volcanoes and frequent typhoons pose ongoing risks to the population

• Filipino and English function as official languages alongside regional languages, reflecting the legacy of Spanish colonial rule lasting over three centuries
