Indonesia: International Briefing

Report date: December 19, 2024

Indonesia's political landscape continues to evolve under President Prabowo Subianto, who has announced ambitious plans to convert all fuel-powered vehicles to electric propulsion [5]. The administration faces significant diplomatic tensions following the death of an Indonesian TNI peacekeeper in a Lebanese Israeli attack, prompting official condemnation from the Foreign Ministry [1][3][5]. Parliamentary voices have emerged calling for the withdrawal of Indonesian forces from the UN peacekeeping mission in Lebanon, reflecting growing domestic pressure over the deployment [5]. Administrative progress continues in infrastructure development, with Minister Basuki Hadimuljono providing testimony on living conditions after one year of residence in the new capital city, Nusantara [1].

Regional governance shows measurable advancement, particularly in West Java where the Human Development Index rose to 75.9 in 2025, exceeding official targets [1]. Local administrative initiatives continue across the archipelago, with village heads in Malang being called upon to activate social welfare posts and update demographic data [1]. Cultural heritage considerations remain active, as Sultan Hamengkubuwono II has been proposed for national hero status with three required criteria outlined by authorities [1]. Religious site management issues have emerged in Pandeglang, where authorities dismantled what they determined to be a false sacred tomb of unclear origin [1].

Economic projections indicate cautious optimism despite various pressures. Bank Indonesia has outlined five strategies to address domestic and international economic challenges while projecting economic growth of 4.9-5.7 percent for 2026 [2]. Gold market performance shows mixed signals, with Antam gold prices declining by 30,000 rupiah on March 30, 2026, though prices remained stagnant across major dealers including Antam, UBS, and Galeri24 [3]. President Prabowo's participation in the Japan-Indonesia Business Forum in Tokyo, following his meeting with the Japanese Emperor, reflects ongoing international economic engagement [1].

Law enforcement agencies have demonstrated enhanced capabilities in tackling financial crimes through technological approaches. Two cyber investigation methods have been employed to uncover online gambling cases and track associated financial flows [1]. A high-profile corruption case has emerged involving videographer Amsal Christy Sitepu, accused of inflating costs for village profile videos in North Sumatra's Karo district, allegedly causing 202 million rupiah in state losses [1][5]. Criminal investigations continue across the archipelago, including the arrest of two suspects in a Bekasi murder case where a victim was found in a freezer [1][3].

Healthcare accessibility has improved through technological integration, with mobile JKN screening features launched to enable participants to conduct independent health assessments [1]. Educational infrastructure faces ongoing challenges, with artificial intelligence utilisation being promoted to address educational technology needs [1]. Transportation patterns during the 2026 Lebaran holiday period showed a 6 percent increase in vehicle numbers compared to previous years, while accident rates declined by 58 percent [1].

Traffic management continues to require active coordination, with one-way traffic policies remaining in effect on Trans-Java toll roads as return flows to Jakarta proceed at measured pace [3]. Transportation authorities report that 22 percent of vehicles have not yet returned to the capital during the post-holiday return flows [3].

Indonesia's national football team is scheduled to face Bulgaria in the FIFA Series 2026 final, representing ongoing international sporting engagement [3]. The team operates under new coach John Herdman, whom defender Kevin Diks has praised for tactical detail and preparation methods [3]. Sports Minister Erick Thohir has expressed confidence in Herdman's capacity to strengthen the national team's performance [3].
