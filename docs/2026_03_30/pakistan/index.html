Pakistan: International Briefing

Report date: December 19, 2024

Pakistan has assumed an active diplomatic role as a mediator between Iran and Washington, facilitating indirect communications between the two parties [1]. Pakistani officials have acknowledged conveying messages in both directions, with the government helping to deliver a 15-point proposal from the United States to Iran [1]. Iran dismissed the initial proposal but responded with counter-proposals, maintaining the diplomatic channel through Pakistani intermediaries [1]. Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar indicated that Turkey and Egypt are also participating in behind-the-scenes diplomatic efforts, suggesting a broader regional mediation framework [1].

Pakistan's educational infrastructure continues expanding through its higher education institutions, with the University of Sindh announcing M.Phil/MS and Ph.D. program admissions for 2026 [4]. The university, established in 1947, offers graduate programs spanning over 30 disciplines across sciences, humanities, and professional fields [4]. Admission requirements include minimum CGPA thresholds of 2.40 for master's programs and 3.00 for doctoral studies, alongside mandatory admission test scores of 50% and 60% respectively [4]. The application portal operates from March 29 to April 16, 2026, with examinations scheduled for May 2-3, 2026 [4].

Pakistani society maintains strong adherence to Islamic principles that shape daily life and social structures [2]. The population follows traditional customs centered around religious observance, with arranged marriages constituting the prevailing matrimonial norm [2]. Family structures emphasize collective decision-making and extended kinship networks, reflecting broader cultural patterns of social organisation [2].

Cultural practices remain deeply embedded in Pakistani daily life, with the shalwar-qameez serving as the national dress worn commonly across both urban and rural areas [2]. Pakistani cuisine centers on chapati or roti as dietary staples, with families typically eating together from shared platters where members consume food from designated portions [2]. The culture emphasizes hospitality toward guests, with hosts expected to ensure visitors feel welcomed during social gatherings [2].

Cricket and field hockey represent Pakistan's most popular sports, maintaining their status as the primary national recreational activities [2]. Traditional rural games persist alongside modern sports, including gulli-danda, kite flying, and marbles, particularly among children in rural communities [2]. These activities reflect the continuation of indigenous recreational practices across generations [2].

Pakistan's cultural heritage encompasses six UNESCO World Heritage sites, reflecting the country's diverse historical and architectural identities [3]. The nation's geographical features include some of the world's highest mountains alongside ancient archaeological sites dating to antiquity [3]. These locations demonstrate the intersection of natural geography and historical significance within Pakistan's territorial boundaries [3].

Approximately 9 million Pakistanis currently reside overseas, forming a significant diaspora population with maintained connections to their country of origin [3]. This international presence represents a substantial portion of the population living beyond Pakistan's borders while retaining cultural and familial ties [3].

Key Developments

• Pakistan has emerged as a diplomatic mediator between Iran and Washington, facilitating indirect communications and message exchanges between both parties

• The country's geographic position and maintained relationships with regional powers have enabled its role as an intermediary in international diplomatic efforts

• University of Sindh has opened 2026 admissions for advanced degree programs across 30+ disciplines, reflecting continued expansion of higher education capacity

• Traditional cultural practices including national dress, dietary customs, arranged marriages, and hospitality norms remain prevalent across Pakistani society

• Cricket and field hockey maintain their status as the nation's most popular sports, while traditional rural games continue in local communities

• Pakistan's cultural heritage includes six UNESCO World Heritage sites and significant archaeological locations demonstrating historical depth
