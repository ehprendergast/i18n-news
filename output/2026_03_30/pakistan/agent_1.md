Pakistan's cultural landscape reflects its Islamic foundation, with the population adhering to religious principles that shape daily life and social structures [2]. The national dress, shalwar-qameez, remains widely worn across both urban and rural areas, representing continuity in traditional attire [2]. Pakistani cuisine centers on chapati or roti as dietary staples, with family meals typically shared from common platters [2]. Social customs emphasize hospitality toward guests, while marriage arrangements continue to follow traditional patterns with family involvement [2].

Cricket and field hockey constitute Pakistan's most popular sports, while traditional games such as gulli-danda persist in rural areas alongside common childhood activities like kite flying and marbles [2]. The country's cultural heritage includes six UNESCO World Heritage sites, reflecting its diverse historical influences [3].
