The pension system faces significant adjustments that will affect future retirees' financial security. New life expectancy tables from the Central Statistical Office (GUS) will result in lower retirement benefits for individuals applying between April 1, 2026, and March 31, 2027 [9]. The Social Insurance Institution (ZUS) calculates monthly payments by dividing accumulated pension capital by projected months of life expectancy [9]. Under the revised calculations, a person with 500,000 złoty in pension capital retiring at age 60 would receive 1,859.43 złoty monthly, compared to 1,876.88 złoty under previous calculations [9].

The pension reform's impact reflects broader demographic pressures facing Polish society. The adjustment mechanism demonstrates how statistical updates translate into concrete financial consequences for citizens approaching retirement. However, ZUS may apply more favorable historical tables if beneficial to individual pensioners [9], providing some flexibility within the new framework.

For workers concerned about reduced pension payments, extending employment remains the primary method to increase future retirement benefits [10]. This approach allows individuals to accumulate additional pension capital while potentially accessing more favorable calculation parameters at older retirement ages.
