# Iran: International Briefing

*Report date: December 19, 2024*

Iran's political landscape has grown increasingly fractured as hardline parliamentarians have branded former foreign minister Mohammad Javad Zarif a "traitor" and accused him of espionage [9]. Lawmakers have urged the judiciary to arrest Zarif alongside former president Hassan Rouhani, reflecting deepening internal divisions within the Islamic Republic's political establishment [9]. The government has simultaneously adopted an aggressive posture regarding infrastructure security, with Foreign Ministry spokesperson Ismail Baghaei pledging Iran will respond "in kind" to attacks on its facilities [6].

Tehran's confrontational stance extends to international forums, where Iran's UN Mission has accused external powers of making "overt and shameless threats" to target civilian infrastructure [6]. Iranian officials have warned they would target similar infrastructure owned or related to adversaries if threatened facilities are struck [6]. This rhetoric coincides with documented strikes on several Iranian industrial and educational facilities, including steel plants at Mobarakeh and Khuzestan [9].

The Iranian economy faces severe disruption as the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively blocked by Tehran, creating significant impacts on global energy markets [7, 9]. Iran has escalated regional tensions by striking Kuwait's oil infrastructure ahead of OPEC+ supply talks [10]. Pakistan has backed a two-phase proposal to halt hostilities that could potentially reopen the strategically vital waterway [9].

Critical infrastructure targeting has affected multiple sectors, with facilities at the Pasteur Institute and Shahid Beheshti University, formerly Melli University, among the sites struck [9]. These developments have transformed daily life across Iran, fundamentally reshaping cities and routines for millions of citizens [9]. The UAE's recent arrest of IRGC-linked money changers potentially signals a broader crackdown on Iran's shadow financial network [16].

Iranian society continues experiencing internal tensions, with authorities executing a fourth protester convicted in connection with a fire at a Basij base in east Tehran during January demonstrations [9]. Human rights organizations have documented at least five executions in the past week linked to anti-government protests and security-related charges [9]. Amnesty International has accused Iran of recruiting children as young as 12 for roles connected to the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps [9].

The ongoing conflict has reached Iran's sporting sphere, with the national football team requesting FIFA to relocate its matches to Mexico amid current conditions [7]. This represents one of the clearest indicators of how extensively the security situation has affected normal cultural and recreational activities throughout the country.

• Regional escalation includes Iranian strikes on Kuwait's oil infrastructure ahead of OPEC+ supply negotiations

• UAE arrests of IRGC-linked financial operatives signal potential broader crackdown on Iranian shadow banking networks
