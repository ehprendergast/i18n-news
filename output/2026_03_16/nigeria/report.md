# Nigeria: International Briefing

*Report date: December 19, 2024*

Nigeria operates as a federal republic under President Bola Ahmed Tinubu, who was inaugurated as the country's 16th president on May 29, 2023 [2]. The government structure distributes power between the federal level, 36 states, and the Federal Capital Territory, with Abuja serving as the capital [2][4]. The legislative branch comprises the Senate under President Godswill Akpabio and the House of Representatives led by Speaker Tajudeen Abbas [2]. Vice President Kashim Shettima completes the executive leadership structure [2]. Tinubu previously governed Lagos State from 1999 to 2007 before assuming the presidency [2]. Government priorities include economic growth, security, and infrastructure development [2].

The Nigerian economy operates across diversified sectors, with services, energy, and agriculture forming the primary pillars [4]. The country's geography, covering approximately 923,769 square kilometres, supports varied economic activities across distinct ecological zones [1]. The Niger Delta region constitutes the heart of Nigeria's oil industry, though this concentration has generated environmental challenges including oil spills and land degradation [1]. Agricultural production varies regionally, with the northern savanna region supporting cattle herding and grain cultivation, while the Guinea Savanna region dominates yam, cassava, and maize cultivation [1]. The Jos Plateau contains tin mining operations, benefiting from the central highlands' mineral resources [1].

Nigerian society encompasses over 250 ethnic groups speaking more than 500 different languages [3]. The Hausa, Igbo, and Yoruba represent the three largest ethnic communities within this diverse demographic landscape [3]. With a population of approximately 225-230 million, Nigeria ranks as Africa's most populous nation and the sixth most populous country globally [4][3]. This demographic diversity manifests in varied cultural practices, art forms, and linguistic traditions across the country's extensive territory.

Nigeria's cultural heritage includes two UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Sukur Cultural Landscape in Adamawa and the Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove in Osun [3]. Traditional craftsmanship continues through institutions such as the Kofar Mata Dye Pit in Kano, established in 1498 and recognised as Africa's oldest dye pit [3]. The country achieved international literary recognition when Wole Soyinka became Africa's first Nobel Laureate in Literature in 1986 [3]. Contemporary artistic expression spans multiple mediums, with recognised artists including Nike Davis-Okundaye, Ben Enwonwu, Bruce Onobrakpeya, and Erhabor Ogieva Emokpae [3].

Nigeria operates the second largest film industry globally and serves as Africa's fashion, technological and creative hub [3]. The country's entertainment sector contributes to its position as a regional cultural centre, with creative industries spanning multiple formats and international reach. Nigeria's biodiversity supports over 1,000 documented butterfly species, with new species continuing to be discovered [3]. This biological diversity reflects the country's varied ecological zones and environmental conditions across its substantial territory.

## Key Developments

• Bola Ahmed Tinubu assumed office as Nigeria's 16th president on May 29, 2023, with government priorities focusing on economic growth, security, and infrastructure development

• Nigeria maintains its position as Africa's most populous nation with approximately 225-230 million inhabitants, ranking sixth globally in population

• The country operates a diversified economy across services, energy, and agriculture sectors, with regional specialisation from Niger Delta oil production to northern grain cultivation

• Over 250 ethnic groups speak more than 500 languages, with Hausa, Igbo, and Yoruba comprising the largest communities

• Nigeria hosts the world's second largest film industry and serves as Africa's fashion, technological and creative hub

• The federal structure encompasses 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory, with legislative leadership under Senate President Godswill Akpabio and House Speaker Tajudeen Abbas

• Cultural achievements include Africa's first Nobel Laureate in Literature, Wole Soyinka in 1986, and two UNESCO World Heritage Sites

• Exceptional biodiversity includes over 1,000 documented butterfly species, with ongoing species discoveries indicating continued biological exploration
