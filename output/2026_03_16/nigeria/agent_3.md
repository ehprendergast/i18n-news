Nigeria operates as a federal republic with power distributed between the federal government, 36 states, and the Federal Capital Territory [2]. President Bola Ahmed Tinubu assumed office on May 29, 2023, serving as the country's 16th president after previously governing Lagos State from 1999 to 2007 [2]. The legislative branch comprises the Senate, led by Senate President Godswill Akpabio, and the House of Representatives under Speaker Tajudeen Abbas [2]. Vice President Kashim Shettima completes the executive leadership structure [2]. The government has identified economic growth, security, and infrastructure development as key priorities [2].

The Nigerian economy operates across multiple sectors, with services, energy, and agriculture forming the primary pillars [4]. The country's diverse geography supports varied economic activities, from oil production in the Niger Delta region to agriculture across different ecological zones [1]. The northern savanna region supports cattle herding and grain cultivation, while the Guinea Savanna belt serves as the mainstay of Nigeria's agricultural economy, dominated by yam, cassava, and maize production [1]. The Jos Plateau in the central highlands contains significant mineral resources, with tin mining representing a major activity in the region [1]. The Niger Delta, characterized by mangrove forests and swamps, constitutes the heart of Nigeria's oil industry, though this has generated environmental challenges including oil spills and land degradation [1].

Nigeria represents a multinational state inhabited by over 250 ethnic groups, with the Hausa, Igbo, and Yoruba comprising the three largest communities [3]. These groups collectively speak over 500 different languages and maintain diverse cultural practices, art forms, cuisines, fashion styles, and festivals [3]. The country has produced notable cultural achievements, including Wole Soyinka, who became Africa's first Nobel Laureate in Literature in 1986 [3]. Nigerian artistic expression spans from historical Benin Kingdom works to contemporary art, with recognized artists including Nike Davis-Okundaye, Ben Enwonwu, Bruce Onobrakpeya, and Erhabor Ogieva Emokpae [3]. The country maintains two UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the Sukur Cultural Landscape in Adamawa and the Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove in Osun [3].

Nigeria hosts the second-largest film industry globally and serves as Africa's fashion, technological, and creative hub [3]. The country possesses exceptional biodiversity, including over 1,000 documented butterfly species, with new species continuing to be discovered [3]. The Kofar Mata Dye Pit in Kano, established in 1498, operates as Africa's oldest dye pit, preserving traditional production methods passed down through generations [3]. Nigeria's population of approximately 225-230 million makes it Africa's most populous nation and the sixth most populous country globally [3][4].
