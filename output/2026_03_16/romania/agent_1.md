Romanian President Klaus Iohannis met with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky in Bucharest, where the two leaders signed a strategic partnership declaration representing the highest level of cooperation between the two nations [3]. The partnership, negotiated over three years, encompasses economic, military, educational cooperation and addresses rights of the Romanian minority in Ukraine [3]. Iohannis acknowledged that historical mistrust between the countries had evaporated since the beginning of the 2022 war, with both nations now assuming mutual responsibility for their shared European region [3]. The leaders also signed a joint declaration on defense material co-production and an energy cooperation document [3].

The automotive sector faces significant challenges as Renault Group relocates production lines from Romania's Dacia plant in Mioveni to other countries [5]. The Dacia Striker model will be manufactured in Turkey, while a new electric A-segment vehicle will be produced in Slovenia rather than at the Romanian facility [5]. The Dacia union attributes these relocations to Romania's unstable economic environment, lack of competitive transport infrastructure, high energy costs, and fiscal uncertainty [5]. The union warns that 1,200 positions will be eliminated in 2026 due to robotization and difficult economic conditions, with the plant's 90% export dependency making it vulnerable to infrastructure and fiscal policy shortcomings [5]. The Mioveni facility directly employs approximately 10,000 workers and supports tens of thousands more through supplier networks [5].

Ukraine and Romania agreed to jointly produce drones and counter-drone systems under the European SAFE defense funding program [3]. Romania will gain access to Ukrainian expertise and technology developed during the conflict, as Ukraine currently manufactures approximately 4 million military drones annually and targets 7 million for 2026 [3]. The countries will construct a 400kV electrical interconnector on the Chernivtsi-Suceava route and modernize the 110kV power line between Porubne and Siret [3]. Plans include developing a vertical gas corridor connecting Greece, Bulgaria, Romania, Moldova, and Ukraine, with potential storage of gases from this corridor and from Romania's Black Sea Neptun Deep project in Ukrainian facilities [3].

President Zelensky addressed Romanian minority rights in Ukraine during the bilateral discussions, providing guarantees regarding continued operation of Romanian-language schools and other minority rights [3]. The Ukrainian president recently signed a decree establishing Romanian Language Day in Ukraine [3]. Political analyst Marius Ghincea from ETH Zurich warns that Romania risks exclusion from Ukraine's reconstruction process despite Zelensky's support, citing the country's inability to capitalize on available opportunities [4].
