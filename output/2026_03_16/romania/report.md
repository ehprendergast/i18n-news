# Romania: International Briefing

*Report date: January 15, 2025*

Romanian President Klaus Iohannis met with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky in Bucharest, where they signed a strategic partnership declaration representing the highest level of cooperation between the two nations [3]. The partnership, negotiated over three years, encompasses economic, military, educational cooperation and addresses rights of the Romanian minority in Ukraine [3]. Iohannis acknowledged that historical mistrust between the countries had evaporated since the beginning of the 2022 war, with both nations now assuming mutual responsibility for their shared European region [3]. During the visit, Zelensky also met with President Nicușor Dan and Prime Minister Ilie Bolojan [3][4].

The bilateral agreements include joint defense material co-production and energy cooperation declarations [3]. Ukraine and Romania will jointly produce drones and counter-drone systems under the European SAFE defense funding program, allowing Romania to access Ukrainian expertise and technology developed during the conflict [3]. Ukraine currently manufactures approximately 4 million military drones annually with a target of 7 million for 2026 [3]. As part of the partnership, Ukraine signed a decree establishing Romanian Language Day in Ukraine [3]. Political analyst Marius Ghincea from ETH Zurich warns that Romania risks exclusion from Ukraine's reconstruction process despite these agreements [4].

Romania's economy faces significant challenges in the automotive sector, as Dacia will eliminate 1,200 positions at its Mioveni facility by 2026 due to robotization and economic conditions [5]. The Dacia Striker model will be manufactured in Turkey instead of Romania, while a new electric A-segment vehicle will be produced in Slovenia rather than at the Romanian facility [5]. These relocations reflect broader competitiveness issues, including the delayed Pitești-Sibiu highway, which has contributed to Romania's declining automotive competitiveness [5].

The Dacia Mioveni plant employs approximately 10,000 workers directly and supports tens of thousands indirectly, with over 90% of its production exported [5]. The facility produces nearly 1,300 vehicles daily at maximum capacity [5]. Dacia will launch a voluntary departure campaign beginning in March with compensation based on employee seniority [5]. The automotive sector's challenges highlight Romania's vulnerability to infrastructure deficits and fiscal policy shortcomings.

Energy cooperation between Romania and Ukraine includes construction of a 400kV electrical interconnector on the Chernivtsi-Suceava route and modernization of the 110kV power line between Porubne and Siret [3]. Plans include developing a vertical gas corridor connecting Greece, Bulgaria, Romania, Moldova, and Ukraine [3]. Gas from Romania's Black Sea Neptun Deep project may be stored in Ukrainian facilities [3]. These infrastructure projects form part of broader regional energy integration efforts.

No significant developments in Romanian society, culture, arts, popular culture, sports, or entertainment were documented in available sources during this reporting period. Cultural institutions and traditional practices continued their operations without notable breakthrough achievements or international recognition emerging from the materials reviewed.

## Key Developments

• Romania and Ukraine signed strategic partnership declaration after three years of negotiations, eliminating historical mistrust established since 2022
• Joint defense production agreement enables Romanian access to Ukrainian drone technology under EU SAFE program
• Energy infrastructure cooperation includes new electrical interconnectors and vertical gas corridor development connecting regional markets
• Dacia announces 1,200 job cuts at Mioveni plant by 2026, with major production lines relocating to Turkey and Slovenia
• Romanian automotive competitiveness declining due to infrastructure deficits, particularly delayed Pitești-Sibiu highway completion
• Ukraine guarantees Romanian minority rights and establishes Romanian Language Day through presidential decree
• Gas storage cooperation planned between Romanian Black Sea projects and Ukrainian facilities
• Political analysts warn Romania risks exclusion from Ukraine reconstruction opportunities despite diplomatic progress
