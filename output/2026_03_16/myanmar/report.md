# Myanmar: International Briefing

*Report date: December 19, 2024*

Myanmar's military junta conducted elections between December 2025 and January 2026, marking a significant political development in the country's ongoing crisis [16]. The electoral process was severely limited in scope, with voting occurring in only 263 of Myanmar's 330 townships [16]. Large conflict-affected regions and displaced populations were excluded from the voting process, fundamentally constraining participation across significant portions of the country [16].

The main opposition National League for Democracy was barred from participating in the elections, eliminating the primary political alternative to military rule [16]. UN officials characterized the elections as failing to restore civilian rule, indicating continued military dominance over Myanmar's political system [16]. The electoral period was marked by sustained military violence, with 408 documented air attacks conducted by Myanmar's military forces [16].

These air strikes killed at least 170 civilians during the election timeframe, demonstrating the parallel continuation of armed conflict alongside the political process [16]. Military operations extended beyond the electoral period, with a junta airstrike on a gold mining area in Kachin State killing dozens [15]. However, resistance forces achieved tactical victories, capturing a military base in Tanintharyi Region after a month-long offensive [15].

The humanitarian situation remains severe, with nearly 5.2 million people displaced both within Myanmar and across international borders [16]. This displacement figure reflects the scale of population movement driven by ongoing conflict and instability throughout the country.

International diplomatic engagement continues despite the crisis, though with controversial elements. Iceland appointed businessman Ko Ko Latt as honorary consul despite his documented links to Myanmar's military conglomerate [15]. This appointment highlights the complex international relationships that persist amid Myanmar's political turmoil.
